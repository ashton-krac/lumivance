# CHAPTER 39: THE SUCCESSION

**DATE:** January 20, 2021  
**LOCATION:** Vice President's Residence, Naval Observatory, Washington, D.C.  
**TIME:** 4:28 PM  
**PRESIDING:** Kamala Harris (The Lotus)  

**ATTENDEES (THE SUCCESSION COUNCIL):**  
1. **Kamala Harris** - *The Lotus*  
2. **Maya Harris** - *The Sister / Counsel / Maternal Desk*  
3. **The AKA Council** - *Pink and Green Network (Public Network / Unaffiliated)*  
4. **Doug Emhoff** - *The Witness in the House (Unaffiliated)*  

---

**I. CALL TO ORDER: THE HEELS COME OFF**

Kamala Harris removed her heels in the hallway and carried them into the room by their straps.

For the cameras she had stood in purple, pearls at her throat, hand raised, face arranged between history and competence. She had smiled while the country said first as though first were an arrival rather than a pressure system. First woman. First Black vice president. First South Asian vice president. First daughter of the particular dead mother whose absence had followed her onto the platform like a second oath.

Now the residence was quiet enough to hear the old house settling.

Doug closed the door.

Maya placed a cardboard box on the table. It contained printed schedules, agency charts, donor calls, security memos, and three recipes in Shyamala Gopalan's handwriting.

"Call to order," Maya said.

Kamala flexed her toes against the rug.

"Good," she said. "Because if one more person calls this a dream, I may prosecute the word."

**II. AGENDA ITEM ONE: THE BRIDGE**

No one said President Biden was temporary with cruelty. Age had already said enough. The question was constitutional, strategic, and spiritual: how to stand one heartbeat from power without looking hungry for breath.

"The office is waiting room and engine room," Maya said. "Most people only see the waiting."

The AKA Council had activated across the campaign: phone banks, rides to polls, group texts, church parking lots, alumni networks, pearls and sneakers turned into civic uniform. Georgia had moved. The Senate had moved. History had moved, though never as far as the slogans claimed.

"They keep calling us a sorority like that makes us decorative," one Council member said.

Kamala laughed. "Let them. Decorations know every room."

Doug, who had learned when not to speak, wrote that down.

**III. SIDEBAR: TOP COP, AUNTIE**

The bind was already waiting.

To some voters, Kamala would be prosecutor, discipline, reassurance, proof that order could wear brown skin and pearls. To others, she would be auntie, code-switcher, kitchen-table laugh, the familiar woman who knows how to say "listen" so a room sits up straighter. Each image offered power. Each image took something.

"They will ask you to be tough on command and warm on command," Maya said.

"They already do."

"And if you refuse?"

"Then I am either cold or unserious."

Maya opened Shyamala's recipe card and read the note written at the bottom:

*Do not let them season you to their taste.*

The room went silent.

Kamala took the card and held it like evidence.

**IV. AGENDA ITEM TWO: THE DIVINE NINE GRID**

The AKA Council displayed the map: sororities, fraternities, HBCUs, alumni chapters, judges, principals, city clerks, doctors, military officers, entrepreneurs, retired aunties who knew more about voter turnout than any consultant with a podcast.

"Not a command structure," the Council member said. "A trust structure."

Maya nodded. "Trust moves where messaging cannot."

"Trust also resents extraction," Doug said carefully.

Everyone looked at him.

He lifted both hands. "I am new, not stupid. If the network only receives asks from Washington, it becomes a mailing list with prettier colors."

Kamala pointed at him. "Keep that."

Kamala and Maya added a reciprocity clause at the Council's insistence: appointments, grants, policy access, student debt attention, maternal health, voting rights defense. Not symbolism sent downward. Material support moving back through the grid.

**V. SIDEBAR: THE MOTHER TONGUES**

The media would flatten her into whichever origin served the segment: Black when convenient, Indian when novel, Jamaican when spicy, immigrant daughter when inspirational, American only when obedient.

Kamala had lived the braid before anyone turned it into copy.

"The Order calls it Third Race," Maya said. "Sometimes I hate the phrase."

"Because it sounds like escape," Kamala said.

"From what?"

"From accountability to the first two. From history. From choosing when choosing costs."

Maya wrote: *Identity cannot be used as solvent.*

Kamala approved the line.

**VI. AGENDA ITEM THREE: READINESS WITHOUT HUNGER**

The 2024 folder sat unopened.

Everyone saw it.

"We prepare because preparation is the office," Maya said.

"Not because we wish the President weak," Kamala replied.

"Correct."

The difference mattered. Ambition had carried men across rivers of blood and earned them statues. Ambition in her body would be diagnosed before it was heard.

Doug said, "What do you need from this house?"

Kamala looked around the room. Residence, symbol, bunker, fishbowl.

"A place where I am allowed to be tired before I become useful again."

The AKA Council member closed her laptop.

"Then that is the first security protocol."

**VII. RESOLUTION: THE LOTUS PROTOCOL**

Kamala and Maya approved the Lotus Protocol, with the public network's conditions attached:

1. Treat the vice presidency as active infrastructure, not passive waiting.
2. Mobilize Divine Nine and HBCU networks through reciprocity, not extraction.
3. Refuse media frames that split discipline from care, Blackness from Indianness, ambition from service.
4. Prepare constitutional readiness for any 2024 scenario without indulging succession fantasy.
5. Protect private fatigue as strategic necessity.
6. Preserve Shyamala's papers outside campaign mythology.

Kamala signed with bare feet planted on the rug.

**VIII. THE OATH OF THE PEARL**

Maya placed Shyamala's recipe card beside Kamala's pearls.

"Do you swear," Maya asked, "to be first without becoming proof that no one else is owed passage?"

"I swear."

"Do you swear," the AKA Council asked, "to remember the line did not begin where cameras noticed you?"

"I swear."

Doug asked nothing. He held out her slippers.

That, too, was entered into the minutes.

**IX. ADJOURNMENT**

Outside, Washington celebrated and fortified itself in the same breath. Fences still stood from the insurrection two weeks earlier. Soldiers slept in marble halls. The republic looked both rescued and feverish.

Kamala went to the window and saw the city darkening into office lights.

The vice presidency had often been described as powerless by men who expected power to announce itself with a throne.

Kamala knew better.

Sometimes power was a room one heartbeat away, a box of papers, a network wearing pearls, and the discipline not to reach before reaching became duty.
