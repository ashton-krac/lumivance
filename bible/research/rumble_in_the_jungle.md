# Rumble in the Jungle

The "Rumble in the Jungle" was a historic boxing event held on October 30, 1974, in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo). It pitted the undefeated heavyweight champion George Foreman against challenger Muhammad Ali. The fight was organized by Don King and funded by Zaire's dictator, Mobutu Sese Seko, aimed at promoting Zaire to the world.

Ali, considered the underdog due to his age and Foreman's raw power, employed a risky and now-legendary strategy called the "Rope-a-Dope." He leaned against the ropes, covering up and allowing Foreman to punch himself into exhaustion, before knocking him out in the eighth round. The event was more than a fight; it was a massive cultural festival featuring top Black musicians (James Brown, B.B. King), celebrating Pan-African unity and Black pride on a global scale.
